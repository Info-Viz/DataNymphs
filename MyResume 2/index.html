MYTHOS IN ArCo

Mapping the Divine: From Olympus to the Underworld, through the ArCo Dataset.

Long, long ago, the world belonged to the Titans. Then, after an exhausting war, the Olympic Gods became victorious and they divided the cosmos into three main areas: Zeus took the reign of the sky, Poseidon the sea, Hades the Underworld, while Earth became the realm of mortals.

This journey descends through these worlds of classical mythology, from the kingdom of Aether into the Tartarus, retracing some of the most important myths.

Through the analysis of the ArCo dataset, while proceeding with the descent, it will be possible to understand the concision between history of art and the classical mythology. Every fresco, every statue and piece of art in Italy tells a story, and our aim is to understand how the ancient world influenced the visual imagery of art even after centuries.

Let the descent begin

AETHER

Above men, hidden between the clouds, rises Mount Olympus.
The 12 main Olympian Gods live here, and from here they rule the cosmos and establish divine laws.
Let’s discover each one of them.

Zeus

King of the Gods and ruler of the sky. Famous for his thunderbolts and great power.

Poseidon

God of the sea and earthquakes. Famous for his trident and for controlling storms.

Hera

Queen of the Gods and protector of marriage. Famous for her nobility and her strong loyalty.

Demeter

Goddess of harvest and the earth. Famous for creating the seasons and the gift of grain.

Aphrodite

Goddess of beauty and love. Famous for her charm and for being born from the sea.

Athena

Goddess of wisdom and strategy. Famous for her intelligence and for helping heroes.

Apollo

God of light, music, and the sun. Famous for his beauty and for telling the future.

Hermes

Messenger of the Gods and guide for travelers. Famous for his speed and his winged shoes.

Hephaestus

God of fire and blacksmith of the Gods. Famous for making magical weapons and tools.

Ares

God of war and physical courage. Famous for his battle skills and warrior spirit.

Artemis

Goddess of the hunt and the moon. Famous for her bow skills and her love for nature.

Dionysus

God of wine, parties, and theater. Famous for bringing joy and celebration to people.
